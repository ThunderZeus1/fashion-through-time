1950s

The New Look

Dior's 1947 New Look set the tone - femininity, cinched waists, full skirts, and gloves. A return to luxury after years of war-time austerity.

  • Silhouette: Hourglass, full skirts, cinched waists
  • Designers: Christian Dior, Coco Chanel, Balenciaga
  • Icons: Audrey Hepburn, Grace Kelly, Marilyn Monroe
  • Defining Piece: The Dior Bar Suit
1960s

The Youthquake

Mary Quant invented the mini skirt. Twiggy became the face of the era. The Mod movement on Carnaby Street rewrote the rules of youth fashion.

  • Silhouette: Mini skirts, A-line shifts, mod suits
  • Designers: Mary Quant, André Courrèges, Yves Saint Laurent
  • Icons: Twiggy, Jackie Kennedy, Brigitte Bardot
  • Defining Piece: The Mary Quant mini skirt
1970s

Bohemia & Disco

The decade of freedom. Bell-bottoms and prairie dresses by day, sequins and Halston jersey by night at Studio 54.

  • Silhouette: Bell-bottoms, jumpsuits, maxi dresses
  • Designers: Halston, Yves Saint Laurent, Diane von Furstenberg
  • Icons: Bianca Jagger, Cher, Jerry Hall
  • Defining Piece: YSL Le Smoking tuxedo
1980s

Power & Excess

Shoulder pads invaded the boardroom. Pop stars wore Versace and Armani. Bigger was better - louder colour, taller hair, bolder logos.

  • Silhouette: Shoulder pads, power suits, leggings
  • Designers: Giorgio Armani, Donna Karan, Gianni Versace
  • Icons: Madonna, Princess Diana, Joan Collins
  • Defining Piece: The Armani power suit
1990s

Minimalism & Grunge

Two extremes ruled side by side: stark minimalism (Helmut Lang, Calvin Klein) and grunge (flannel, ripped jeans). And the rise of the supermodel.

  • Silhouette: Slip dresses, slip skirts, oversized denim
  • Designers: Calvin Klein, Helmut Lang, Alexander McQueen
  • Icons: Kate Moss, Naomi Campbell, Cindy Crawford
  • Defining Piece: The Calvin Klein slip dress
2000s

Y2K & Logomania

Low-rise jeans, velour tracksuits, and It-bags. The decade that confused everyone, and that Gen Z would obsessively revive twenty years later.

  • Silhouette: Low-rise jeans, micro-skirts, layered tanks
  • Designers: Tom Ford for Gucci, Marc Jacobs, Stella McCartney
  • Icons: Paris Hilton, Sarah Jessica Parker, Beyoncé
  • Defining Piece: The Juicy Couture tracksuit
2010s

Streetwear & Athleisure

Hoodies became couture. Off-White, Supreme, and Yeezy redefined luxury. Instagram turned every street corner into a runway.

  • Silhouette: Skinny jeans → athleisure, normcore, oversized
  • Designers: Virgil Abloh, Phoebe Philo, Riccardo Tisci
  • Icons: Rihanna, Kim Kardashian, Zendaya
  • Defining Piece: Yeezy sneakers
2020s

Sustainability & Revival

Vintage and resale boomed. Genderless and size-inclusive collections moved to the centre. Y2K came back. So did corsets.

  • Silhouette: Low-rise revival, oversized blazers, slip skirts
  • Designers: Daniel Roseberry, Pierpaolo Piccioli, Harris Reed
  • Icons: Bella Hadid, Harry Styles, Zendaya
  • Defining Piece: The Bottega pouch
