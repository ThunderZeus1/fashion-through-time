Chapter III

Designers & Icons

Meet the makers. Discover the categories. From haute couture ateliers to streetwear drops - fashion's heroes await.

Explore the Categories

Click a category to read about its origin, defining traits, and key names.

Haute Couture

The pinnacle of fashion. Hand-sewn in Parisian ateliers, made-to-measure for individual clients, and legally protected by the Chambre Syndicale. Each piece can take hundreds of hours to construct.

Era of dominance: 1858–Present

Key Houses: Chanel, Dior, Valentino, Schiaparelli, Givenchy

Ready-to-Wear

Standard-sized clothing produced in advance - the engine of the modern industry. YSL democratised it with Rive Gauche in 1966; today it accounts for the majority of every luxury house's revenue.

Era of dominance: 1960s–Present

Key Designers: Yves Saint Laurent, Calvin Klein, Phoebe Philo

Streetwear

From California skateparks and New York hip-hop to global luxury. Supreme, Off-White, and Yeezy taught the runway to queue. The hoodie became couture.

Era of dominance: 1990s–Present

Key Designers: Virgil Abloh, James Jebbia, Shawn Stussy, Kim Jones

Avant-Garde

Clothing as art. The Japanese movement of the 80s (Comme des Garçons, Yohji Yamamoto) and McQueen's theatrical runways turned the catwalk into a stage for ideas - beauty, decay, identity, protest.

Era of dominance: 1980s–Present

Key Designers: Alexander McQueen, Rei Kawakubo, Martin Margiela

Minimalist

"Less is more." Clean lines, neutral palettes, perfect fit. Born in 90s Antwerp and Calvin Klein's New York, revived by Phoebe Philo at Céline, and the foundation of today's "quiet luxury".

Era of dominance: 1990s–Present

Key Designers: Calvin Klein, Helmut Lang, Phoebe Philo, Jil Sander

Sustainable

Fashion's most urgent conversation. From Stella McCartney's two-decade leather-free run to deadstock-only brands and the resale boom - the defining ethics question of the 2020s wardrobe.

Era of dominance: 2010s–Present

Key Designers: Stella McCartney, Gabriela Hearst, Marine Serre

Legendary Designers

Filter by category to find your favourite legends.

Coco Chanel

Couture

Liberated women from the corset. Invented the little black dress, costume jewellery, and the No. 5.

Christian Dior

Couture

Restored Paris to the top of fashion with 1947's New Look - and built the house that still leads couture today.

Yves Saint Laurent

Ready-to-Wear

Le Smoking, the safari jacket, ready-to-wear at Rive Gauche. Made the modern wardrobe.

Audrey Hepburn

Icon

Givenchy's muse. The black dress in Breakfast at Tiffany's defined elegance for an entire century.

Twiggy

Icon

The face of the 60s Youthquake. Made the mini skirt and Mary Quant household names.

Karl Lagerfeld

Couture

Revived Chanel, ran Fendi for half a century, and made the white ponytail a global brand.

Vivienne Westwood

Streetwear

Mother of punk. Took torn t-shirts to the streets and helped build the attitude behind modern streetwear.

Alexander McQueen

Avant-Garde

Tailor of genius and the most theatrical mind in fashion. Every show was a story.

Tom Ford

Ready-to-Wear

Saved Gucci in the 90s, revived YSL, then built his own house. The high priest of glossy luxury.

Donatella Versace

Ready-to-Wear

Kept her brother's legacy alive - bold, golden, unmistakeably Versace. Queen of the supermodel runway.

Naomi Campbell

Icon

One of the original supermodels. Walked the runway for every major house - and still does.

Anna Wintour

Icon

Editor-in-chief of Vogue since 1988. The bob, the sunglasses, the front row - the most powerful figure in fashion.
