Chapter IV

Craft & Innovation

From the hand-stitched couture atelier to algorithmic supply chains and lab-grown leather - how clothing has been made, and the shifts that changed everything.

A Timeline of Craft

Five inventions that reshaped how the world dresses.

1846 - Today

Sewing Machine

Elias Howe's patent. The single invention that took clothing from artisan craft to industrial production.

1858 - Present

The Couture Atelier

Charles Frederick Worth opens the first Parisian house. The atelier model - designer, premier d'atelier, petites mains - still defines couture.

1939 - Today

Synthetic Fibres

DuPont's nylon. Then polyester, lycra, spandex. The materials that made affordable mass-produced clothing possible.

1990 - Today

The Global Runway

Fashion Week becomes televised, then live-streamed. Paris, Milan, New York, London - a single annual calendar drives a global industry.

2015 - Today

Sustainable Tech

Lab-grown leather, recycled polyester, on-demand production. The shift from "newer, cheaper, faster" to "fewer, better, slower".

Then vs. Now

How making and wearing clothes has changed in a single generation.

Then (1950s - 1990s)

  • Two collections per year - Spring/Summer and Autumn/Winter
  • Garments tailored to last decades, often passed down
  • Visit a tailor, or buy from a department store
  • Magazines tell you what to wear; collections drop slowly
  • Most pieces sewn at a sewing machine in a domestic factory

Now (2010s - Today)

  • Up to 52 micro-drops a year from fast-fashion retailers
  • Average garment worn 7 times before being discarded
  • Shop on your phone; clothes arrive in 24 hours
  • Instagram and TikTok set trends in real time
  • Production scattered globally; supply chains span 12+ countries

Iconic Garments

Six pieces that changed how the world dressed.

1947

The Bar Suit

Christian Dior · The New Look

The cream silk jacket and pleated black skirt that relaunched Paris couture and defined post-war femininity.

1961

The Little Black Dress

Givenchy · Breakfast at Tiffany's

Hubert de Givenchy's design on Audrey Hepburn - the silhouette that made the LBD a non-negotiable wardrobe staple.

1966

Le Smoking

Yves Saint Laurent

The tuxedo redesigned for women. Sharp, sensual, and at the time, scandalous.

1980

The Power Suit

Giorgio Armani

Soft tailoring with broad shoulders. The uniform that took women from the secretarial pool to the boardroom.

1994

The Slip Dress

Calvin Klein

Bias-cut satin in a single sleek line. Underwear became evening wear, and minimalism took over the 90s.

2015

The Yeezy Sneaker

Kanye West & Adidas

A streetwear shoe that made luxury houses queue. The moment hype culture and high fashion fused.
